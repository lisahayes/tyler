Leak Detection & Repair in Tyler, TX 75702

ZIP 75702 covers northeast and older north Tyler, a band of mid-century neighborhoods built as the city pushed outward from its historic core. Slab construction dominates, with some pier-and-beam on the older end, and the plumbing is a generation behind the premium south side.

Mid-century north Tyler

The 75702 ZIP takes in the established north and northeast neighborhoods that filled in during Tyler's mid-century growth. These are mostly slab-on-grade homes with copper supply from the 1950s through 1970s, with a few pier-and-beam houses on the closer-in streets. The service lines running from the city meter to these homes are often original galvanized, now corroding from the inside.

The mid-century copper is old enough to be in the early-to-middle pinhole window, and the galvanized service lines are the first thing we check when a 75702 home loses pressure across the whole house.

The leaks this ZIP produces

Service-line and supply leaks from aging galvanized lead here, followed by slab and copper-pinhole leaks in the mid-century homes. Older cast-iron drains crack and slow. A whole-house pressure drop, a warm spot on the floor, or a drain that backs up slowly are the everyday 75702 signs.

Because the plumbing is reaching the later part of its life rather than the very end, many 75702 homes are in the spot-repair zone, though we watch for the pattern that points toward a repipe.

Locating leaks in this era of construction

We confirm a service-line leak at the meter and trace it acoustically. Slab and supply leaks get acoustic and thermal detection for a small, precise repair. Old drains get a camera. The goal is the same as anywhere: find the spot, open only what the repair needs.

Old and new systems on the same street

The 75702 ZIP is mixed enough that neighbors can have very different plumbing. An original galvanized service line next door to an updated copper supply means the right question is always about the specific home, not the street. We read the home's age and ask about any updates before we arrive, so the right tool comes out.

Questions from this part of Tyler

My north Tyler home is from the 1960s. What pipes does it likely have?

Most mid-century 75702 homes have copper supply and cast-iron drains. The service line from the meter may still be the original galvanized. We confirm on site.

How do I know if it is the service line or a slab leak?

We check the meter first. With the house valve closed, a meter that still moves means the loss is between the meter and the house, out in the service line.

Is aging copper worth repairing or should I repipe?

One isolated pinhole is a repair. A pattern of several failures is when a repipe earns its cost. We give you both numbers honestly.

My drain runs slowly and keeps coming back. What is it?

Likely an old cast-iron drain that has corroded or cracked. A camera shows the break so we fix the cause, not just clear it again.

How do I reach you in 75702?

Call (903) 651-5125 any hour and describe what you are seeing, and we will head out.

Is 75702 at risk for slab leaks too?

Yes. The mid-century slab homes here sit on the same expansive clay as the rest of Tyler, and the copper lines bonded to those slabs flex with the seasonal movement. A slab leak is on the table alongside the service-line and supply issues common in this ZIP.
