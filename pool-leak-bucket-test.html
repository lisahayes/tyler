Pool Leak or Just Evaporation? Tyler Pool Bucket Test Calculator

Tyler's long swimming season means pool owners often notice a dropping water line but cannot tell whether it is a leak or normal East Texas evaporation. The bucket test is the most reliable way to find out without calling a technician first. Enter your measurements and get an instant answer.

How to run the bucket test

  1. Fill a 5-gallon bucket with pool water to about 1 inch from the top.
  2. Place the bucket on the first or second pool step, submerged just enough so the water inside the bucket matches the pool level outside.
  3. Mark the water level inside the bucket and the pool water level outside with tape or a marker.
  4. Run the pool pump on its normal schedule. Do not use the auto-fill. Wait 24 hours.
  5. Measure how much the level dropped inside the bucket (evaporation) and outside the bucket (pool). Enter both below.

Pump-on vs pump-off follow-up test

After your first test, run a second test with the pump off for 24 hours and compare:

  • Larger loss with pump ON: the leak is likely in the return plumbing (pressure side).
  • Larger loss with pump OFF: likely the skimmer or suction-side plumbing.
  • Equal either way: likely the shell, fittings, or a liner tear.
