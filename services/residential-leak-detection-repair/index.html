Residential Leak Detection & Repair in Tyler, TX

A leak in your own home is personal. It is the bedroom carpet, the kitchen you cook in, the bill you actually pay. Residential leak work in Tyler is as much about protecting the living space as it is about the plumbing running underneath it.

Every Tyler home leaks a little differently

Tyler's housing stock spans more than a century, and the era of the home shapes the leak. The mixed-age streets of Cumberland and Mid Tyler hold everything from mid-century slabs to remodeled older homes, and University Place near UT Tyler mixes 1970s construction with newer infill. We read the home before we read the leak.

A pre-1950 pier-and-beam house hides its plumbing in a crawl space and tends toward galvanized and cast iron. A 1990s slab hides it in concrete and runs copper. The same wet ceiling means different things in each, and the detection method changes to match.

Detection that respects the house

The whole point of modern detection is keeping your home intact. We confirm the leak with a meter and pressure test, then pinpoint it with acoustic listening, tracer gas, and thermal imaging before anything opens. The mark goes on the wall, ceiling, or floor only once we know the failure sits behind it.

That matters more in a lived-in home than anywhere. A precise locate means one small access cut in the guest bath instead of an exploratory hole in three rooms, and it means the family is back to normal sooner.

Repairs scaled to the failure

Residential repairs run the full range, and we match the fix to what we find. A worn fixture or a loose fitting is a quick job. A failed supply line is a spot repair or a reroute. A cracked drain may call for a camera and a trenchless liner. A home with failing copper throughout is a candidate for a PEX repipe.

You get the findings and the cost for each path before any work begins, with no pressure toward the biggest option. The goal is the right repair for your home, not the largest invoice.

Protecting the home around the leak

Water does collateral damage fast in a house, and Tyler humidity speeds mold along once moisture sits behind a surface. The faster the source is found and stopped, the less the leak takes with it, which is the whole argument for detection over demolition.

A leak found while it is still a faint stain is a plumbing repair. The same leak found after it has fed a wall cavity for weeks is a plumbing repair plus drywall, paint, and remediation.

Questions Tyler homeowners ask

Do you handle older Tyler homes and newer ones?

Both, and we read them differently. A pre-1950 pier-and-beam home with galvanized lines gets a different approach than a 1990s slab with copper. The detection method follows the house.

How disruptive is the work?

As little as the leak allows. Pinpointing the source first means a small access cut over the failure instead of exploratory holes across several rooms.

What does a residential visit cost?

It depends on what the leak turns out to need, and you hear the number before any work starts. Call (903) 651-5125 and we will walk you through what to expect.

Can you tell me whether it is worth a full repipe?

We will give you the honest picture. One isolated failure is a spot repair. A pattern of failures across the home is when a repipe earns its cost, and we price both.

Can a small leak really hurt my home that much?

In Tyler humidity it can. Moisture trapped behind a wall or under a floor can start mold within a few days, so a leak that looks minor today becomes a drywall and remediation job if it sits. Finding it early is what keeps it small.

Do I need to be home for the detection?

It helps for access and so we can show you what we find, but we can often work with whatever access you arrange. Call (903) 651-5125 and we will set up a window that fits your day.
