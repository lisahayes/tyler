About Tyler Leak Repair Experts

We are a leak detection and repair service built for one city. Tyler homes have a particular set of problems, from expansive clay under the slabs to aging copper in the older suburbs, and we would rather know that ground deeply than spread thin across half of East Texas.

Why we focus on finding before fixing

A leak you cannot see is easy to get wrong. Crews used to open a wall or a floor on a hunch, and when the guess missed, the homeowner paid for the hole anyway. We built this service around the opposite idea. Locate the leak first with acoustic listening, tracer gas, and thermal imaging, narrow it to a few inches, and only then open the one spot that matters.

That approach fits Tyler especially well. So many leaks here trace back to foundation movement in the expansive clay, where a slab rides the seasonal swing of the soil and a copper line eventually shears at a joint. Pinpointing that failure saves the floor above it.

A little Tyler context

Tyler was founded in 1846, named for President John Tyler, and grew into the regional center of the East Texas Piney Woods. It carries the nickname Rose Capital of America, hosts the Texas Rose Festival every fall since 1933, and keeps the Tyler Rose Garden as one of its landmarks. We mention all of that as the community we work in, not as anything we are affiliated with.

The plumbing side of the city is just as layered. Most of Tyler drinks surface water, about 96% drawn from Lake Tyler, Lake Tyler East, and Lake Palestine, treated at the Golden Road Water Treatment Plant since 1951. It runs moderately hard at around 4.2 grains per gallon. Knowing the water and the soil is half of knowing why a given home leaks.

How we operate

Every call routes to a licensed Texas plumber who works leaks day in and day out. We give a real estimate before the work starts, we do not fabricate awards or review counts to look established, and we hold a Texas plumbing license through the TSBPE.
